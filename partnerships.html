World of Hospitality / Partnerships

Bring your brand to Qatar. Don't bring the headaches.

We're the on-the-ground operating partner for established F&B concepts looking at Doha. Your brand stays yours. The local complexity becomes ours.

Who this is for.

You've built a restaurant or a group that works at home. You're being asked — by guests, by press, by your own commercial team — about opening in the Gulf. Qatar is on the shortlist. But the operational unknowns are real: license structures, landlord dynamics, hiring at standard, supply chain reliability, the cost of getting any of it wrong.

This is where we come in. We're not a consultancy that hands you a deck. We're an operator that signs the lease, hires the team, and takes accountability for the P&L. Our job is to make Qatar feel like a known market for you — because for us, it is.

What you get when you work with us.

Three things that consistently decide whether a new market launch succeeds or stalls.

01 / Local power

Real relationships across Doha.

Landlords, regulators, suppliers, recruiters, contractors. Not LinkedIn relationships — the kind built over years of opening rooms.

02 / Brand discipline

We don't dilute what you built.

Our existing partners trust us with their flagship concepts because we treat the original brief like scripture. Adaptation, not reinvention.

03 / Operating capability

The day-to-day is handled.

You get monthly numbers you can trust and a single point of accountability for everything between the storefront and the back office.

How a typical partnership unfolds

Stage 01

Introduction call

You tell us about the brand, where it's at, and what you're imagining for the Gulf. We tell you honestly whether we think it works and how we'd approach it. No deck, no pitch theatre.

Stage 02

Market & feasibility

If both sides want to keep going, we run a focused feasibility — locations, positioning, financials, opening timeline, capital structure. Usually 6–10 weeks.

Stage 03

Commercial structure

We agree the right model — franchise, management contract, or joint venture — sized to the brand's ambitions and risk appetite. Heads of terms in writing before anything else moves.

Stage 04

Build & open

Site, fit-out, licensing, hiring, training, launch. We run on a published timeline with weekly reporting back to your team.

Stage 05

Operate & grow

The venue opens, we run it, you see the numbers. When the first location proves out, we plan the next one together.
